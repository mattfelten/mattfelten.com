## The Problem

Mission Cloud's Technical Account Managers run recurring check-ins with 30+ customers each. Before Collaboration Loops, every meeting meant manually assembling a Google Slides deck, pulling data from Gainsight, Vantage, ConnectWise, and Slack, then rebuilding it from scratch the following month. Customers arrived to meetings without context. Historical decisions evaporated between calls. There was no shared record either side could return to.

The core tension: the tools that existed treated these meetings as one-sided reporting events. What we needed to build was a space where both Mission's team and their customers could prepare, collaborate, and look back together.

## The Design Challenge

The obvious solution was a better template, a structured document TAMs could fill out instead of building slides. We deliberately didn't build that.

A document is static. It captures a moment, then goes stale. What we needed was something that stayed alive between meetings: pulling fresh data from multiple systems, preserving what was decided last time, and giving customers a reason to come back on their own rather than waiting to be invited.

This meant designing a blueprint-and-widget architecture where each section of a loop could display live data, be configured per meeting type, and behave differently depending on whether a Mission team member or a customer was viewing it, while still feeling like a single coherent space.

## Key Decisions

### Blueprints over free-form templates

We could have built a generic document builder. Instead we invested in purpose-built blueprints, TAM check-in, CSM success planning, general meeting, each with pre-wired data connections. The constraint creates consistency across 30+ customer relationships per TAM and makes onboarding faster than a blank canvas would.

### Live data that freezes on close

Widgets display current data while a loop is open, then snapshot that state when the meeting closes. This preserves historical accuracy. You can see exactly what the Cloud Score looked like in February, not what it looks like today. A subtle decision that changes the archival value of every loop significantly.

### Manual creation over automation for launch

We deliberately scoped out auto-generated loops for the beta. TAMs creating loops themselves meant they had intent and ownership. Automating it risked loops being ignored because nobody asked for them. Adoption over elegance.
